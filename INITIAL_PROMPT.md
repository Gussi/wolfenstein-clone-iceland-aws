# "Pots & Parliament" (working title): game concept brief

## Premise

A Wolfenstein 3D style raycaster shooter that satirizes Icelandic institutional and political dysfunction. You play an ordinary citizen in the aftermath of an unnamed financial Crash ("the Hrun"), climbing through a bloating parliament-ministry complex to force a reckoning at the top office. The building grows new committee wings as you advance, which is both the level-expansion mechanic and a running joke about bureaucratic sprawl.

The game reads as satire of systems and archetypes. It never attacks identifiable real people or real political parties.

## Fixed creative decisions

1. Combat is nonlethal slapstick. You stun, disperse, and humiliate enemies with escalating kitchenware, echoing the real Kitchenware Revolution protests. No gore, no killing.
2. The protagonist is a bewildered everyman just trying to file one form, not an angry partisan. The tone mocks all sides.
3. Satire targets structural and historical material (the Crash, a concentrated fishing-quota system, an offshore-finance scandal that toppled a government). Abstractions only, zero real names or party identifiers.

## Weapons

Wolf3D's knife-to-chaingun arc, reskinned: wooden spoon, frying pan, pot-and-ladle (an area-stun "noise" crowd-clearer), then a finale weapon, the "Búsáhöld Barrage," that summons a crowd of fellow protesters banging pots. The climax is collective action, not a minigun.

## Enemies (all abstractions)

- Red Tape: swarming cannon fodder.
- Form-Pushers: basic ranged clerks who fling rejection stamps.
- Spin Doctors: project a cloud that scrambles controls or the minimap.
- Lobbyist-blobs: slow, drop money pickups when popped, spawn more enemies if ignored.
- Útrás-men: elite inflated floating raiders abstracting the pre-crash banker class.

## Bosses (each tied to a structural grievance)

- Quota-Kraken: a net-monster hoarding all the fish.
- Offshore Vault: teleports its own health bar into hidden rooms you must find and smash before you can damage it.
- The Establishment: final boss that, when beaten, splits into three smaller coalition versions that immediately start fighting each other.

## Secrets and pickups

Push-walls hide cronyism back rooms: the minister's nephew hires, no-bid contracts, a deal-making sauna. The score counter is "connections," ranked against "everyone's cousins." Health pickups are coffee and kleinur.

## Texture and tone

Fast arcade movement, grid-based maze levels, push-wall secrets, instant nonlethal-stun feedback. Distinctly Icelandic small-country gags: enemies threaten to tell your mother, endless identical committee rooms, a perpetual half-finished construction wing. Recurring background gag: one volcano erupting in the windows across every level, ignored by everyone inside.

## Hard content rule

Every political reference stays at the level of archetype or historical structure. No real person, living or dead, and no real party identifiable by name, likeness, slogan, or unmistakable detail.
